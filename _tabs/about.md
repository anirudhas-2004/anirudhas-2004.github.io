# About Me

I'm Anirudh A S, a final-year Electronics and Communications Engineering student at PES University. I'm interested in communication systems, VLSI design, and quantum networking.

## Academic Focus & Research

My main interests are in **communication systems** and **VLSI design**, with a growing interest in **quantum networking**. Currently, I'm working on my thesis involving the simulation of quantum network links for entanglement distribution under real atmospheric conditions.

I find quantum networking interesting because of its unique capabilities and the way it changes how we think about information transfer. The properties of quantum entanglement and its applications in secure communication are areas I'm exploring more.

## Technical Background

My work spans different areas—from FPGA implementations for aerospace applications to analog circuit design and electromagnetic simulations. I use tools like MATLAB, Vivado, CST, and Qiskit to work on various projects.

My projects include time synchronization systems, frequency divider circuits, and quantum recursive network architectures. Each project has taught me something different about the field.

I'm also interested in the semiconductor industry and the engineering behind chip manufacturing. The precision required in modern fabrication processes is something I find impressive.

## Beyond Engineering

When I'm not working on projects, I play badminton and enjoy traveling. These activities help me unwind and often give me new perspectives on life.
